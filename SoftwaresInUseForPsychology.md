
Psychology uses a wide range of software for research, therapy, assessment, neuroscience, education, and statistics. Here's a categorized guide.

## 1. Statistical Analysis Software

Used for analyzing psychological research data.

* IBM SPSS Statistics – Most common in psychology research.

  * ANOVA
  * Regression
  * t-tests
  * Factor analysis
  * Reliability (Cronbach's alpha)

* R

  * Free and open source
  * Advanced statistics
  * Machine learning
  * Data visualization

* JASP

  * Free
  * Bayesian statistics
  * User-friendly interface

* Jamovi

  * Free
  * SPSS-like interface
  * Ideal for students

* Stata

  * Clinical research
  * Longitudinal analysis

* SAS

  * Large-scale healthcare research

* MATLAB

  * Signal processing
  * Cognitive modeling

---

## Best software by goal

| Goal                  | Recommended software      |
| --------------------- | ------------------------- |
| Beginner statistics   | Jamovi, JASP              |
| Professional research | IBM SPSS Statistics, R    |
| Cognitive experiments | PsychoPy, E-Prime         |
| Clinical practice     | SimplePractice, TheraNest |
| Qualitative research  | NVivo                     |
| Brain imaging         | SPM, FSL                  |
| EEG research          | EEGLAB, MNE-Python        |
| Surveys               | Qualtrics                 |

If your goal is to master psychology from beginner to researcher level, a strong progression is:

1. Jamovi or JASP
2. IBM SPSS Statistics
3. R
4. PsychoPy
5. NVivo
6. Python with scikit-learn for computational psychology and AI-driven research.

This combination covers most of what is used in modern academic psychology, cognitive science, clinical research, and computational psychology.
