1. Psychological Effects Everyone Should Know
   
1. Confirmation Bias
People tend to notice and remember information that supports what they already believe.
2. Halo Effect
One positive quality (such as attractiveness or confidence) can make someone seem smarter, kinder, or more competent.
3. Anchoring Effect
The first number or idea you hear influences later judgments, even if it's arbitrary.
4. Framing Effect
People react differently depending on how the same information is presented.
5. Mere-Exposure Effect
Repeated exposure to something often makes people like it more.
6. Social Proof
People often look to others' behavior to decide what they should do.
7. Loss Aversion
Most people feel the pain of losing more strongly than the pleasure of gaining an equivalent amount.
8. Reciprocity
When someone does something for us, we often feel a pull to return the favor.
9. Scarcity Principle
Things seem more valuable when they appear limited or hard to get.
10. Cognitive Dissonance
When actions and beliefs conflict, people often change one to reduce the discomfort.
