

**Paper Title:** *Artificial Intelligence Based on Cognitive Psychology: Research, Applications and Future Directions* (Frontiers in Neuroscience, 2022) 

## Overall Idea (Simple Explanation)

The paper argues that **today's AI is intelligent but not psychologically intelligent**.

Current AI can:

* Recognize patterns
* Play games
* Translate languages
* Detect faces

But it **doesn't truly understand emotions, intentions, desires, or human mental states**.

The authors propose that **AI should be combined with Cognitive Psychology** to create more human-like intelligence capable of:

* Understanding emotions
* Showing empathy
* Making human-like decisions
* Communicating naturally with humans 

---

# Main Research Question

The authors ask:

> **Can AI become more intelligent if we teach it how the human mind works instead of only how the brain processes information?**

Current AI copies the **brain**.

Future AI should copy the **mind**.

---

# Central Thesis

Current AI mainly imitates:

Brain
↓
Neurons
↓
Learning
↓
Memory

But humans are much more than neurons.

Humans have

* emotions
* beliefs
* motivations

These come from **cognitive psychology**.

Therefore:

> AI + Cognitive Psychology = Better AI

The paper has five major parts:

Explains why current AI is incomplete.

Problem:

Current AI learns patterns but cannot genuinely understand psychological experiences.

Example:

Hence psychology is necessary.

These all attempt to bring psychology into AI.

### 3. Three Major Applications

This is the core of the paper.

The authors analyze three application areas.

---

## Application 1 — Facial Attractiveness

Question:

Why do humans think some faces are attractive?

The authors explain that attractiveness is **not completely random**.

Many people agree on attractiveness across cultures.

Researchers built AI systems that learn these patterns using datasets like **SCUT-FBP5500**.

AI extracts:

* facial symmetry
* geometry
* skin texture
* facial proportions

Models used include:

* AlexNet
* ResNet
* ResNeXt

Performance reached high correlations (around 0.8–0.85), suggesting AI can learn aspects of human aesthetic judgment. 

**Meaning:**

AI is learning one aspect of human perception.

---

## Application 2 — Affective Computing

This is one of the most important sections.

Question:

Can AI detect emotions?

Humans express emotions through:

* face
* voice
* posture
* EEG brain signals

AI analyzes these signals to infer emotional states.

Examples include:

* Facial Action Coding System (FACS)
* Speech emotion recognition with LSTM/Bi-LSTM
* EEG-based emotion classification
* Multimodal depression detection combining speech and facial expressions

The paper reports improved recognition when combining modalities, illustrating the promise of emotionally aware AI. 

Possible uses:

* Depression screening
* Driver monitoring
* Human-computer interaction
* Mental health support

---

## Application 3 — Music Emotion

Question:

Can AI understand how music changes emotions?

The paper discusses systems that:

* Analyze a user's emotional state (e.g., via physiological signals)
* Generate or recommend music
* Attempt to move the user toward a target emotional state (such as calm or happiness)

Potential applications include:

* Music therapy
* Education
* Personalized music generation

# Why Psychology Matters

According to the authors, psychology contributes abilities that today's AI lacks:

| Traditional AI      | Cognitive Psychology  |
| ------------------- | --------------------- |
| Logic               | Emotions              |
| Pattern Recognition | Motivation            |
| Memory              | Beliefs               |
| Classification      | Intentions            |
| Optimization        | Empathy               |
| Statistics          | Subjective Experience |

Their message is that AI should combine both.

# Strengths

* Connects psychology and AI in an accessible way.
* Covers multiple real-world applications.
* Surveys a broad range of related research.
* Highlights emotionally intelligent AI as an important direction.

---

# Limitations

The authors also acknowledge important limitations:

* Emotions differ across cultures and individuals.
* Mental states are difficult to measure objectively.
* Much of the supporting evidence comes from existing studies rather than new experiments.
* Current AI still does not truly "feel" emotions—it recognizes patterns associated with them.

## Final takeaway

The central message of the paper is:

> **Current AI can compute and recognize patterns, but future AI should also model human cognitive processes such as emotion, intention, and empathy. Integrating cognitive psychology with AI could lead to more natural, human-centered intelligent systems.** 

If you're studying AI or CSE, this paper serves as a **high-level survey** rather than introducing a new algorithm. It is valuable for understanding why many modern research areas—such as affective computing, explainable AI, and socially intelligent AI—draw heavily from cognitive psychology.
